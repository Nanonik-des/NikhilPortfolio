Tatya — Horror Survival
Lead Designer · GDD · Level Design · Puzzle Design

Game Brief

Tatya is a first-person horror survival game focused on exploration, psychological tension, puzzle solving, and item-based progression. The player explores a cursed environment while helping Tatya escape from a prison magically sealed using mantras.

The game combines atmosphere-driven horror with layered puzzle systems, exploration mechanics, progression-based gameplay, and environmental storytelling.

Responsibilities

  • Designed the complete gameplay progression and player flow.
  • Created item-based puzzles and environmental interactions.
  • Designed level layouts and room progression systems.
  • Created and maintained the complete GDD documentation.
  • Worked on horror pacing, fail states, and checkpoint systems.

Phase 1 — Initial Concept

The early concept focused heavily on psychological horror and mystery. The initial direction explored cursed environments, exploration-based gameplay, and survival mechanics.

Phase 2 — Puzzle Design

The gameplay evolved into a puzzle-heavy horror experience with item-based progression, clue systems, environmental interactions, and layered room design.

Phase 3 — Gameplay Flow & Iteration

During iteration, the game flow was refined to improve pacing, player guidance, puzzle readability, and exploration structure.

Phase 4 — Final Horror Experience

The final version focused on atmosphere, environmental storytelling, sound tension, progression balancing, and immersive horror presentation.

Before & After — Design Iterations

Multiple gameplay systems, room layouts, and interaction flows were redesigned throughout development to improve horror pacing and player readability.

Die Away — Puzzle Strategy
Game Designer · Systems Design · Puzzle Design

Game Brief

Die Away is a grid-based puzzle strategy game where the player controls a dice that moves according to the number shown on its top face.

The idea originally came from a sudden prototype concept phase where I planned multiple experimental puzzle games. Later, I revisited the idea and developed the game with a team within 3 days.

Responsibilities

  • Designed the core dice movement system.
  • Created gameplay and puzzle logic.
  • Designed progression and level structure.
  • Worked on UX and gameplay readability.

First Idea

The original concept focused on orientation-based movement where every move affects future possibilities.

Final Iteration

The final version improved readability, path planning, and overall player flow.
